Art of Mindful Breathing

Mar 27, 2026 • 3 min read

Ever felt the world around you is too much intimidating? No matter what you do, what you think, it is always scary.

The constant feeling of an irritation inside your chest, not able to breathe, or maybe you can, but just not enough to reach till the bottom of your lungs.

We call it Anxiety.

Why Breathing Changes Everything

The objective is to give your pre frontal cortex (PFC) the control of your brain from your limbic system.

🧠 What's happening inside The limbic system is your brain's alarm center — it triggers anxiety, fear, and that tight-chest feeling. Your prefrontal cortex (PFC) is the rational, calm part. Slow, intentional breathing is one of the fastest ways to shift control back to the PFC. You are literally rewiring the moment.

Lets walk it through together, how a simple mindful breathing can help you realize that you are OK, you are FINE.

Let's Begin

Let's take a long breath in, hold it, then release it. It is not simple, it takes time, patience and practice.

Focus on your body, feel where it hurts, or where it is uncomfortable. If possible keep your palm on your chest and feel yourself taking a deep breath.

Breathe

Follow the circle — let it guide your breath. Expand with it, hold, then release slowly.

Now, I want you to focus on the breath that you take. Here is the pattern to follow:

⏱ The 6 – 4 – 6 Breathing Technique

6
Inhale
Count 1 to 6 slowly
4
Hold
Count 1 to 4, stay still
6
Exhale
Count 1 to 6, release

Tip: Don't listen to your heartbeat — we make this mistake often, trying to sync our heartbeat with seconds, and it will only make you more uncomfortable. Instead, try tapping your fingers, or your legs, or anywhere on your body which feels comfortable, to keep a count of seconds.

Repeat the whole process again for at least 10 times. It will be hard, but practicing it at your comfort zone every day will help you do it during stressful situations.

The Advanced Version

There is also an advanced version of it — you will do what you are doing, just modify it a little bit. You have to visualize the breath going in, from your nose, then to lungs, then to the bottom of the lungs.

I hope you feel better :)
